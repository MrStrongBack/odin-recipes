Tacos Al Pastor

Description

This is an easy to follow at home recipe used at your favorite taco truck near you. The vast majority of ingredients used in this recipe might already
be in your pantry or just a grocery store trip away. So enjoy this recipe and happy eating!

Ingredients

  • 5 lb boneless pork shoulder
  • 3 tablespoods achiote paste
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 cup pineapple juice

Steps

  1. Slice the pork shoulder into about 1-centimeter (¼ in) slices, then transfer to a large dish or bowl. In a small bowl,
    combine the achiote paste, garlic powder, cumin, salt, pineapple juice, and chili powder. stir and mash until
    the ingredients are smooth with no lumps inside the bowl. pour the bowl over the pork slices, then toss
    around the pork and the marinade until the pork is fully covered. Proceed to cover the pork with a plastic wrap,
    refrigrate it for at least 2 hours.
  2. Preheat the oven to 350°F(180°C). Wrap a baking sheet with Parchment paper or aluminum foil.
  3. Place a slice of pineapple on the baking sheet. Take a skewer and push it directly in the middle of the pineapple.
    Remove the pork from the fridge and push the slices through the skewer, layering the pork, and pineapple one after another.
    Ensure that there is a one inch gap from the top of the skewer and push one more pineapple slice on the very top.
  4. Bake for 1hr-15mns to 1hr-30mns, until the pork is slightly burnt on the outside and deep red. Rest for 10 minutes to allow
    the meat to finish cooking and cool down for the serving process.

For Serving

  • 10 small corn tortillas
  • 1 white onion
  • 1 cup of chopped cilantro
  • 1 cup of prefered salsa
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges

Serving Steps

  1. Place a portion of pork on the tortillas.
  2. Followed by a few pieces of pineapples, some onions and cilantros.
  3. Now the the piece that really brings the recipe together, add a spoonful of salsa with diced avocado.
  4. Congratulations!, you have made a delicious mexican delicacy. Enjoy!
